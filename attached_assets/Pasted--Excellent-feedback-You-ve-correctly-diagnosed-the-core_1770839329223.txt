### ✅ **Motion Specs for Clarity**

The principle here is "calm utility." Motion should feel responsive and natural, not decorative or distracting.

**1. Page Load & Entrance**
*   **Hero Elements (Headline, Sub-headline, CTAs):** Fade in sequentially with a slight upward slide. Duration: ~600ms, Easing: `ease-out`. This creates a sense of deliberate arrival.
*   **Background:** A very subtle, slow shift in gradient color or opacity over 30+ seconds. No rotation or fast movement. It should feel like breathing, not spinning.

**2. Scroll-Based Interactions**
*   **"How It Works" Zone Cards:** As the user scrolls, each zone card (Journal, Research, Thinking Partner) fades in and slides up from ~20px. This reveals information progressively and rewards scrolling.
*   **Parallax (Use Sparingly):** Apply a very subtle parallax effect to the background gradient or a soft geometric layer behind the main content. The foreground text and cards should not move. This adds depth without compromising readability.

**3. Hover & Interaction States**
*   **CTA Buttons:** On hover, the button's background color should smoothly transition (e.g., 250ms `ease-in-out`). Add a very subtle box shadow to create a "lift" effect.
*   **Zone Cards:** On hover, the card's border or icon color should change, and it could scale up by 1-2%. This provides clear, immediate feedback that the element is interactive.
*   **Text Links:** On hover, the underline should animate in from left to right.

**4. What to Remove Immediately**
*   **All Spinning/Rotating Elements:** This is the #1 priority. Replace any continuous rotation with a static design or a subtle, one-time entrance animation.
*   **Unnecessary Background Animations:** If a moving element doesn't guide the eye or provide feedback, remove it. Clarity is paramount.

By implementing these changes, the DJZS homepage will shift from a mysterious art piece to a clear, compelling invitation to a better thinking tool. It will feel stable, trustworthy, and immediately useful.
