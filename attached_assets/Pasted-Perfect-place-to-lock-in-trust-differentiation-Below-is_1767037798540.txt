Frequently Asked Questions

⸻

How is DJZS different from ChatGPT or Claude?

DJZS is not a chat app and not a general AI assistant.

ChatGPT and Claude are centralized AI platforms:
	•	Your conversations happen on their servers
	•	Memory is global or platform-controlled
	•	The primary interaction is asking questions

DJZS is a local-first thinking system:
	•	You write first, in your own workspace
	•	The AI responds only when you ask it to
	•	Memory is explicit, user-controlled, and local
	•	There are no feeds, profiles, or engagement loops

DJZS helps you think.
It doesn’t replace your thinking.

⸻

What does “decentralized” mean in DJZS?

Decentralized does not mean “stored on a server”
and it does not mean “everything is on a blockchain.”

In DJZS, decentralized means:
	•	Your data is not dependent on a single company
	•	Your thinking does not live in a central database
	•	Ownership starts on your device, not a platform

Centralized servers — and public blockchains — are still single points of failure.
They can be indexed, surveilled, or compromised.

In DJZS, decentralization starts with local-first storage.
Everything else is optional.

⸻

Is my data really private?

Yes — by design.
	•	Your journal and research live on your device first
	•	Data is encrypted end-to-end before it ever leaves your machine
	•	DJZS does not scan, profile, or monetize your writing
	•	Nothing syncs unless you choose to sync it

DJZS works fully offline.

If your device is offline, your thoughts are offline.

⸻

How does AI memory work in Journal and Research?

The AI does not have automatic or global memory.

DJZS uses explicit, user-controlled memory:
	•	You write an entry
	•	You click Think with me
	•	The agent sees:
	•	Your current entry
	•	Up to 3 memory pins you previously saved
	•	The response is generated and stored locally as an insight

If something is worth remembering, the AI may suggest a memory —
but only you can pin it.

There is no background memory.
There is no silent learning.

⸻

What’s the difference between Journal mode and Research mode?

They use the same system, but different thinking lenses.

Journal mode
	•	Summary
	•	Insight
	•	Reflection question

Research mode
	•	Key claims
	•	Evidence
	•	Unknowns
	•	Next question

The AI adapts its output — not your data.

⸻

What is a Memory Pin?

A Memory Pin is a thought you choose to keep.
	•	Pins stay on your device
	•	Pins are reused as context in future sessions
	•	Pins are never added automatically

This allows your thinking to compound over time
without creating a centralized memory graph.

⸻

Why not store everything on a centralized server or blockchain?

Because that’s not decentralized — it’s just outsourced risk.

Centralized servers:
	•	Are attractive targets for breaches
	•	Require trust in a company
	•	Turn private thinking into stored assets

Public blockchains:
	•	Are permanent and indexable
	•	Are not designed for private cognition
	•	Can expose metadata even if content is encrypted

DJZS uses local-first storage and treats cloud or blockchain as:
	•	Optional
	•	Secondary
	•	User-initiated

Your thoughts don’t belong on a global ledger by default.

⸻

Can I sync or back up my data?

Yes — when you choose to.

You can:
	•	Export your data
	•	Back it up
	•	Timestamp or prove ownership using decentralized networks

But DJZS never requires this.

The system works without sync.
Sync exists for your convenience, not control.

⸻

Who owns my data?

You do.
	•	You write it
	•	You store it
	•	You choose what’s remembered
	•	You choose what’s shared
	•	You can leave at any time with your data intact

DJZS is a tool you own — not a platform that owns you.

⸻

What is a “Zone”?

A Zone is a thinking context.

Zones help you separate:
	•	Journaling
	•	Research
	•	Reflection
	•	Long-term memory

Each Zone changes how the AI responds,
without mixing your thoughts into a feed or profile.

You move through Zones.
You don’t perform in them.
