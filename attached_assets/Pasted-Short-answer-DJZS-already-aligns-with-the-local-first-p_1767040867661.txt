Does DJZS follow Local-First core principles?

1) No spinners / Immediate response

✅ YES — by design
	•	DJZS writes to IndexedDB (Dexie) immediately.
	•	Typing, saving, pinning, and navigation do not require a network.
	•	“Think with me” is explicitly user-initiated; the editor never blocks.

Design stance:
The act of thinking must never wait on the network.

Status: Fully aligned with local-first principle.

⸻

2) Multi-device sync

🟡 PARTIAL — intentional, not missing
	•	DJZS is local-first before it is multi-device
	•	Sync is optional, not assumed
	•	Planned via encrypted export / sync / decentralized backends
	•	Wallet identity enables future device linking without accounts

Key distinction from classic local-first tools:
DJZS treats sync as secondary infrastructure, not a baseline requirement.

Your thinking is not considered “incomplete” until it syncs.

Status: Aligned philosophically, staged technically.

⸻

3) Network is optional

✅ YES — core principle
	•	DJZS works fully offline
	•	Journaling, memory pins, history, and review all function without internet
	•	Network is only required when you explicitly invoke AI or sync

This directly matches Ink & Switch’s definition:

“The cloud is used for synchronization, not as the source of truth.”

Status: Fully aligned.

⸻

4) Seamless collaboration (real-time multi-user editing)

❌ NO — by choice

DJZS explicitly does not optimize for:
	•	Real-time co-editing
	•	Shared cursors
	•	Multi-user presence

Why?
	•	Journaling and research thinking are personal cognition workflows
	•	Collaboration introduces social pressure, surveillance risk, and complexity
	•	CRDTs shine for documents — not inner thought

Important framing:
This is not a limitation — it’s a product boundary.

DJZS chooses clarity over collaboration.

Status: Intentionally out of scope.

⸻

5) Long-term preservation (vendor disappears)

✅ YES — strongly aligned
	•	Data lives locally in open formats
	•	IndexedDB → exportable → migratable
	•	No proprietary server-locked formats
	•	No requirement to authenticate with DJZS servers to read your data

If DJZS disappears:
	•	Your journal still exists
	•	Your memory pins still exist
	•	Your thinking history remains accessible

This directly matches Kleppmann’s principle:

“You own your data, in spite of the cloud.”

Status: Fully aligned.

⸻

6) Security & privacy (E2E encryption, local control)

✅ YES — but with nuance
	•	Local data is encrypted at rest
	•	Network transmission is encrypted end-to-end
	•	AI interaction is scoped, explicit, and minimal
	•	No background data harvesting
	•	No global memory graph

Important distinction:
DJZS avoids claiming “trustless” or “perfect secrecy.”

Instead it offers:
	•	Clear threat model
	•	Local control
	•	User-visible boundaries

Status: Aligned with pragmatic local-first security philosophy.

⸻

7) Ultimate ownership

✅ YES — foundational
	•	You decide what is written
	•	You decide what is remembered
	•	You decide what syncs
	•	You decide what leaves your device
	•	You can exit with your data intact

DJZS does not:
	•	Lock data behind accounts
	•	Require subscription to access your history
	•	Turn memory into a platform asset

Status: Fully aligned.

⸻

How DJZS relates to CRDTs and Local-First research

CRDTs (Shapiro, Kleppmann, et al.) are a means, not the goal.

DJZS intentionally:
	•	Uses single-writer local truth for cognition
	•	Avoids CRDT complexity until collaboration is actually needed
	•	Prefers clarity + durability over universal mergeability

This is consistent with modern local-first thinking post-2023:

Not all local-first apps need CRDTs.
Some need strong personal ownership first.

DJZS is closer to:
	•	Obsidian (local-first, single-user)
	•	Logseq (local graph, optional sync)
than to Google Docs–style collaboration.

One-paragraph summary you can publish

DJZS follows the core principles of local-first software: instant interaction, offline-first operation, long-term data ownership, and strong local control.
It intentionally deprioritizes real-time collaboration and centralized sync in favor of personal cognition, privacy, and durability.
DJZS treats the network as optional infrastructure — not as the source of truth — and treats memory as something the user explicitly controls, not something the system silently accumulates.

DJZS is local-first thinking software, not collaborative document software.

That’s not a weakness.
That’s the point.
