## 🔥 What This Interface Does (The Big Picture)

This is **not** a “set and forget” trading bot.

It’s a **thinking interface** where you:
- **Build** a thesis before risking capital
- **Stress test** it with AI before execution
- **Sign & track** every trade as an artifact
- **Learn** from every outcome (win or lose)

You’re not automating decisions — you’re **automating intelligence**.

## 🧱 Breakdown of the Interface Components

### 1. **Build Trade Thesis**
This is where you codify your trade idea — not just “buy ETH”, but “why, when, how much, and what would prove me wrong”.

#### Fields:
- **Asset**: ETH, BTC, SOL… — the token you’re trading
- **Position Size %**: 2% — your risk allocation (never risk more than 2-5% per trade)
- **Side**: BUY/SELL — directional bias
- **Timeframe**: SHORT/MEDIUM/LONG — your holding horizon
- **Conviction (5/10)**: Slider — quantify your confidence (forces honesty)
- **Entry Type**: MARKET/LIMIT — execution style
- **Take Profit % / Stop Loss %**: Optional — automated exit triggers
- **Invalidation**: “What would invalidate this thesis?” — your escape hatch

> 💡 This section is where your **Thinking Partner** should be most active — challenging your assumptions before you commit.

---

### 2. **Market Conditions** (Optional but Powerful)
This is where you contextualize your thesis with real-time or projected market data.

#### Fields:
- **Current Price**: Manual input — forces you to check the market before committing
- **Sentiment**: FEAR/NEUTRAL/GREED — your macro view (or AI-synthesized)
- **Catalysts**: “ETF approval, halving, protocol upgrade” — key events that could move the asset
- **Notes**: Your reasoning, thoughts, context — your private journal entry tied to this trade
- **Memo**: Short description — for quick recall later

> 📈 This section connects to your **Research Zone** — you can paste in research summaries, charts, or links to your dossiers.

---

### 3. **Workflow: Compose → Stress Test → Risk & Sign → History**

#### ✍️ Compose
- Fill out the thesis
- Add market context
- Write your notes
- Set your conviction level

#### 🧪 Stress Test
- Let Thinking Partner challenge your thesis
- Ask: “What’s the counter-argument?”
- Ask: “What’s the worst-case scenario?”
- Ask: “Is this thesis based on recent noise or long-term fundamentals?”

#### 🛡️ Risk & Sign
- Confirm position size is within your risk limits
- Validate stop loss/take profit levels
- Sign the trade artifact — this is your **commitment to discipline**

#### 📜 History
- All past trades are logged
- You can review your conviction vs. outcome
- You can see which catalysts mattered
- You can track your emotional patterns (FOMO, panic, overconfidence)

## 🚀 Next Steps: Turning This Into a “Bot”

This interface is already the **brain** of your trading bot.

To make it autonomous, you need to add:

### 1. **Execution Layer**
```typescript
// In server/openclaw.ts
class TradingExecutor {
  async executeTrade(thesis: TradeThesis): Promise<TradeResult> {
    // 1. Validate with ThinkingPartner
    const stressTest = await this.thinkingPartner.stressTest(thesis);
    if (!stressTest.approved) {
      throw new Error(`Thesis failed stress-test: ${stressTest.reasons}`);
    }

    // 2. Check risk parameters
    const riskCheck = await this.riskManager.validate(thesis);
    if (!riskCheck.withinLimits) {
      throw new Error(`Risk limits exceeded: ${riskCheck.violations}`);
    }

    // 3. Execute trade
    const result = await this.wallet.execute({
      asset: thesis.asset,
      side: thesis.side,
      amount: thesis.size,
      price: thesis.entryPrice
    });

    // 4. Log to Journal Zone
    await this.journalInsight.logTrade(result, thesis);

    return result;
  }
}
```
